What is Cardiac Perfusion?

Cardiac perfusion refers to the flow of blood through the coronary arteries to the heart muscle (myocardium). It ensures the heart receives enough oxygen and nutrients to function properly. Adequate perfusion is vital for maintaining healthy heart tissue and preventing conditions like angina or heart attacks. It is often evaluated using imaging techniques such as myocardial perfusion scans.

Who is a Cardiac Perfusionist?

A cardiac perfusionist is a specialized healthcare professional who operates the **heart-lung machine** during open-heart surgeries. They manage the patient's **circulation and respiratory functions** while the heart is stopped, ensuring oxygenated blood continues to flow throughout the body. Perfusionists also monitor blood pressure, temperature, and other vital signs during the procedure. They play a critical role in surgeries like **coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)** and **heart valve repair or replacement**.

Cardiopulmonary Bypass (CPB) Machine

The CPB machine, or cardiopulmonary bypass machine, is used during open-heart surgery to take over the functions of the heart and lungs. It pumps blood through the body, oxygenates it, and removes carbon dioxide while the heart is stopped. This allows surgeons to operate on a still and bloodless heart. The machine also helps maintain the patient's body temperature during the procedure. A cardiac perfusionist is responsible for operating the CPB machine.

Components of the CPB Machine

Cardiotomy Reservoir

Collects blood suctioned from the surgical field and removes large particulates, air, and debris...

Venous Reservoir

Serves as the collection chamber for deoxygenated blood returning from the patient...

Oxygenator

Functions as the artificial lung of the bypass circuit. Enriches venous blood with oxygen...

Oxygenator with Integrated Filter

Combines gas exchange with arterial filtration in one unit. Captures microemboli and particulates...

Cardioplegia Set

Delivers cooled cardioplegia solution to arrest the heart safely. Reduces myocardial metabolism...

System Panel

The centralized command station for the CPB circuit. Displays real-time data on pressures...

Pressure Controller

Maintains safe and consistent arterial line pressure. Prevents damage to patient vessels...

Level Controller

Monitors reservoir levels continuously. Automatically stops the pump to prevent air entrainment...

Bubble Detector

Continuously scans for microbubbles in the blood path. Triggers alarms or stops the pump...

Bubble Trapper

Physically captures air bubbles within the blood circuit. Removes residual gas before delivery...

Roller Pump

Drives blood through the circuit using rotating rollers. Gently compresses tubing to generate flow...

Centrifugal Pump

Generates flow via a spinning impeller. Reduces shear stress and provides non-pulsatile flow...

Temperature Monitor

Continuously tracks patient and circuit blood temperature. Enables precise heating/cooling...

Heater-Cooler Machine

Controls blood and cardioplegia temperature during bypass. Supports myocardial protection...

Tubings

Connects all circuit components with medical-grade materials. Maintains sterile blood pathways...

Connect HL7

Interfaces the CPB machine with hospital systems using HL7 standards for data exchange...
